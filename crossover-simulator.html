💡 What's Happening?

A passive crossover sits between your amplifier and the two drivers. It uses only coils and capacitors — no power of its own — to send each driver the range of frequencies it handles well.

A capacitor passes highs and blocks lows, so a series cap feeds the tweeter. An inductor (coil) passes lows and blocks highs, so a series coil feeds the woofer. That's the whole trick.

Order = how steeply it rolls off. 1st order (one part) = 6 dB/octave, gentle. 2nd order (two parts) = 12 dB/octave, steeper and cleaner separation.
