What Am I Looking At?

Voltage (V) is the electrical pressure the battery supplies — how hard it pushes charge around the loop.

Current (I) is the rate of flow of charge, measured in amps. One amp ≈ 6.24 × 10¹⁸ electrons passing a point every second.

Resistance (R) opposes the flow. Electrons collide with the vibrating metal ions, giving up energy as heat.

Power (P) is energy delivered per second — what lights the bulb or warms the wire.

💧 Water analogy: Voltage is the height of the tank, current is how much water flows, resistance is a narrow pipe. Raise the tank (voltage) or widen the pipe (lower resistance) and more water flows.
